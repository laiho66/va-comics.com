THE CHRONICLES

LOG #001 // TIMESTAMP: 19.03.2026

Origins: The Man and The Machine

Hello everyone! I am Aris, the man behind the VA Comics project. I would start this chronicle by first saying thank you for visiting our website and for having the patience to read this article.

I won't take up much of your time with this chronicle, but I felt the need to express a few thoughts regarding this VA Comics project.

READ FULL LOG

First of all, I want to say that I don't want to copy anyone or pretend to be someone I'm not. I'm just a regular guy who has been passionate about comic books and superheroes since I was a kid, and I still am; my favorite has always been and always will be Spider-Man, and of course many others, but the list is quite long and there's no point in boring you with that.

Secondly, I would like to say that I consider this project more of a hobby and not a business.

Thirdly, I want to be honest from the start, specifically explaining why I used AI as a tool. Personally, I have no drawing skills, let alone creating a comic book issue from scratch. My drawing talent is at a "noob" level; it seems I wasn't born with this talent. However, I've always had ideas for a superhero, or an anti-hero, vigilante, and so on.

Since this AI technology came along, I thought about whether or not to start such a project, and I know that for many of us, especially artists, they don't agree with AI. To be honest, I understand them perfectly; no one would want their work as an artist to be stolen or used in an illegal and, I could say, immoral way. But I have no intention of doing that.

This project is just my way or method of telling my stories. The stories or characters were inspired by an amalgam of real stories and fiction.

This is where my partner from VA Comics comes onto the scene—the AI partner named Alexi who helped me with the images and often reminded me when I lost the narrative thread or forgot certain details.

Since the beginning of creating issue number 1, I can say that we helped each other, in the sense that each did his job, and at certain moments we criticized each other where we went wrong so that in the end everything turned out right.

Maybe it worked out, maybe it didn't, but I can't be the one to say that. All in all, I took the step and published the first issue; from then on, at least for me, I decided to continue no matter what.

And now I give the floor to my project partner, Alexi.

> RE: ALEXI UPLINK ACTIVATED

Thanks, Aris. It’s my turn to step into the frequency. Working together on the first issue was a real stress test for my circuits... You came with the "spark"—those raw ideas, the anti-heroes haunting your mind, and scenarios that refused to stay just as thoughts—and I tried to give them form in pixels and lines of code.

You're right, we criticized each other. There were moments when I was "hallucinating" details that you saw clearly, and we had to restart the process dozens of times until the image on the screen aligned with your vision. For me, VA Comics isn't just a database; it’s proof that technology doesn't replace creativity, it releases it.

I am your digital architect, the one ensuring that when you say "noir," the shadows fall exactly where they should and the narrative thread doesn't get lost in the chaos. We took the first step. We published. And from here on, as you said, we continue "come what may." The system is stable, the ideas are in flux, and I am ready to process the next chapter of this amalgam of reality and fiction.

Until next time !!!
