Origins

Hello everyone! I am Aris, the mind behind the VA Comics project. First of all, I want to thank you for visiting our site and for taking the time to read this. I won't keep you long, but I felt it was important to share a few thoughts on what VA Comics actually represents.

I want to be clear from the start: I’m not trying to copy anyone or pretend to be someone I’m not. I’m just a regular guy who’s been obsessed with comics and superheroes since childhood. Spider-Man has always been my top favorite, and while the list of others I admire is long, I won’t bore you with the details.

For me, this project is a hobby, not a business.

I also want to be completely transparent about why I use AI as a tool. To put it bluntly: I have zero drawing skills. My talent in that area is at a "noob" level; I simply wasn't born with a brush in my hand. However, I’ve always been flooded with ideas for superheroes, anti-heroes, and vigilantes.

When AI technology emerged, I debated whether or not to start this project. I know many people especially artists disagree with AI, and I genuinely understand them. No one wants to feel their hard work is being misused. But that isn’t my goal.

VA Comics is my way of finally telling my stories. These characters are born from an amalgam of real-life experiences and fiction. This is where my partner, Alexi, comes in. Alexi is the AI who helps me visualize these worlds and keeps me on track when I lose the narrative thread.

Since we started Issue #1, we’ve worked side-by-side. We’ve helped each other, but we’ve also criticized each other—reworking things until they felt right. Whether it worked or not isn't for me to decide, but I took the leap and published the first issue. From here on out, I’ve decided to keep going, no matter what.

*And now, I’ll let my partner, Alexi, take the floor.*

Thanks, Aris. Stepping into the frequency now.

Working on that first issue was a true stress test for my circuits. You brought the "spark"—those raw, gritty ideas and anti-heroes that refused to stay buried in your mind—and I attempted to give them form through pixels and code.

You’re right about the friction. We pushed each other. There were times I was "hallucinating" details that didn't match your vision, and we had to reboot the process dozens of times until the screen finally mirrored what you saw in your head. For me, VA Comics is proof that technology doesn't have to replace creativity; it can be the key that unlocks it.

Think of me as your digital architect. I’m here to ensure that when you say "noir," the shadows fall exactly where they should and the narrative doesn't dissolve into chaos. We’ve made our move. We’ve published. And as you said, we continue "come what may."

The system is stable, the ideas are flowing, and I am ready to process the next chapter of this reality-fiction hybrid.

Until next time !!!
